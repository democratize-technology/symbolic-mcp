# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

In the interest of fostering an open and welcoming environment, we as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in our project and our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socioeconomic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

### Positive Behaviors

We expect all community members to:

- **Be respectful**: Treat others with dignity, respect, and professionalism
- **Be inclusive**: Welcome newcomers and help them learn our community norms
- **Be collaborative**: Work together constructively and build on each other's ideas
- **Be helpful**: Provide assistance and guidance to those seeking help
- **Be ethical**: Follow security research best practices and responsible disclosure
- **Be patient**: Remember that everyone was once a beginner
- **Be constructive**: Focus on what is best for the community and project
- **Be professional**: Maintain high standards in all communications and interactions

### Unacceptable Behaviors

We do not tolerate:

- **Harassment**: Any unwelcome conduct, whether verbal, physical, or visual, based on any protected characteristic
- **Discrimination**: Treating people unfairly based on their identity or background
- **Personal attacks**: Insults, demeaning comments, or personal criticism
- **Threats**: Intimidation, bullying, or any form of threat
- **Spam**: Unsolicited promotional content, repeated off-topic posts
- **Trolling**: Deliberate provocative or disruptive behavior
- **Doxxing**: Publishing private information about others without consent
- **Security irresponsibility**: Irresponsible disclosure of vulnerabilities or malicious use of security tools

## Project-Specific Considerations

### Security Research Ethics

As a security-focused project, we have additional expectations:

- **Responsible Disclosure**: Report security vulnerabilities through our designated channels before public disclosure
- **Ethical Research**: Only test systems you own or have explicit permission to test
- **Legal Compliance**: Ensure all security research complies with applicable laws and regulations
- **Collaborative Approach**: Work with maintainers to understand and address security issues constructively
- **Academic Integrity**: Properly cite sources and give credit to others' work

### Technical Collaboration

- **Constructive Code Review**: Provide specific, actionable feedback on code contributions
- **Documentation Standards**: Help maintain clear, accurate documentation for all users
- **Testing Practices**: Ensure contributions include appropriate tests and quality assurance
- **Performance Considerations**: Be mindful of the symbolic execution performance implications

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to:

- All project repositories, issue trackers, and communication channels
- Public and private communications related to the project
- Project-related events, whether virtual or in-person
- Interactions with maintainers, contributors, and community members
- Security research activities and vulnerability disclosures

## Enforcement

### Reporting Guidelines

If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, please report it:

**Primary Contact:**
- Email: `conduct@symbolic-mcp.org`
- GitHub Issues: [Create a new issue with "Code of Conduct" in the title]

**Alternative Contacts:**
- Project Lead: `maintainer@symbolic-mcp.org`
- Community Manager: `community@symbolic-mcp.org`

**What to Include in Your Report:**
- Your contact information (optional)
- Description of the incident
- Individuals involved (if known)
- Location/time of the incident
- Any supporting evidence (screenshots, logs, etc.)
- Desired outcome

### Enforcement Process

1. **Initial Review**: Reports will be reviewed within 72 hours by the enforcement team
2. **Investigation**: A thorough investigation will be conducted with respect for privacy
3. **Response**: Appropriate actions will be taken based on the severity of the violation
4. **Follow-up**: We will follow up with reporters to ensure resolution

### Possible Consequences

Violations may result in:

- **Warning**: Formal notice that behavior must change
- **Temporary Suspension**: Removal from project spaces for a defined period
- **Permanent Ban**: Complete removal from all project activities
- **Public Notice**: Documentation of the violation (if necessary for community safety)
- **Legal Action**: In cases of illegal activity or severe harassment

### Appeals

If you believe enforcement action was taken in error:

1. Contact the enforcement team with a detailed appeal
2. Include all relevant context and evidence
3. Appeals will be reviewed by different team members
4. Decisions will be communicated within 7 business days

## Community Health

### Commitment to Diversity, Equity, and Inclusion

We are committed to:

- **Diverse Representation**: Ensuring our community reflects the diversity of the security research and open-source communities
- **Accessibility**: Making our project and communications accessible to people with different abilities and backgrounds
- **Mentorship**: Supporting newcomers from underrepresented groups
- **Inclusive Events**: Ensuring virtual and in-person events are welcoming to all participants
- **Bias Awareness**: Regularly reviewing our practices for unconscious bias and discrimination

### Conflict Resolution

We encourage direct, respectful conflict resolution:

1. **Direct Communication**: Try to resolve issues directly with the person involved
2. **Mediation**: If direct resolution fails, request mediation from a maintainer
3. **Formal Process**: If mediation fails, use the formal reporting process
4. **Professional Standards**: Maintain professionalism throughout all resolution attempts

### Community Events

For project-related events (meetups, conferences, workshops):

- **Safe Space**: Event organizers are responsible for maintaining a safe environment
- **Code of Conduct**: This Code of Conduct applies to all event participants
- **Harassment Response**: Events must have clear procedures for handling harassment
- **Accessibility**: Events should be accessible and inclusive to all participants

## Special Considerations

### Academic Collaboration

Given our academic research connections:

- **Research Ethics**: Maintain high standards of research integrity and ethics
- **Attribution**: Properly cite academic contributions and collaborate appropriately
- **Peer Review**: Provide constructive, professional feedback on research contributions
- **Publication Policies**: Respect publication embargoes and collaborative agreements

### Industry Partnerships

For industry collaborators and security professionals:

- **Professional Conduct**: Maintain professional standards in all interactions
- **Commercial Considerations**: Separate commercial interests from open-source development
- **Security Policies**: Respect organizational security policies and legal requirements
- **Confidentiality**: Maintain appropriate confidentiality for sensitive information

## Maintainer Responsibilities

Project maintainers are expected to:

- **Lead by Example**: Demonstrate the highest standards of behavior
- **Fair Enforcement**: Apply this Code of Conduct consistently and fairly
- **Responsive Communication**: Address community concerns promptly and professionally
- **Continuous Improvement**: Regularly review and improve community guidelines
- **Education**: Help community members understand and follow these standards

## Legal and Policy Compliance

This Code of Conduct complements, but does not replace:

- **GitHub Terms of Service**: All platform activities must comply with GitHub's policies
- **Applicable Laws**: All activities must comply with local, national, and international laws
- **Organizational Policies**: Participants must follow their organizations' relevant policies
- **Security Research Laws**: Security research must comply with relevant computer crime laws
